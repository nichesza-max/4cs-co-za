01

Durban chain retail

Gateway, The Pavilion, and La Lucia Mall carry the standard SA retail set: mall-based jewellery chains as part of the Foschini Group, a national jewellery chain, and smaller independent jewellers. For a Durban buyer who wants a finished engagement ring, a gift piece, or branded jewellery with financing, the KwaZulu-Natal retail corridor is well-stocked. Store staff are trained on the product range. Brand warranties apply.

02

What Durban's retail market does not offer at specialist level

Durban's diamond market does not carry a significant specialist dealer presence comparable to Johannesburg's Bedfordview zone. For buyers who need a GIA-grade comparison across multiple loose diamonds, a discussion of investment value, or a valuation for estate or insurance purposes, the chain retail environment does not fully address those needs.

Direct answers

Common questions

Are there specialist diamond dealers in Durban?

Durban has independent jewellers and chain retailers, but a dedicated stone-first specialist dealer comparable to Prodiam in Johannesburg is not a prominent feature of the Durban market. For specialist work, Durban buyers typically engage Johannesburg or Cape Town specialists.

Can Prodiam help a Durban buyer remotely?

Yes. Email sales@prodiam.co.za or call +27 11 334 9010. Prodiam can assess a stone's certificate data, provide a market-rate reference, or advise on a purchase specification remotely before any travel is necessary.

Is a national jewellery chain in Durban a good option for an engagement ring?

a national jewellery chain, Natal Wholesale Jewellers, carries finished engagement rings at accessible price points. It is a national chain with a legitimate retail track record. For a design-first, budget-conscious purchase, it is a practical Durban option.

How does insured stone movement work for out-of-province buyers?

Prodiam can advise on secure and insured stone delivery for SA buyers who need to move a stone between cities. Contact the Bedfordview office to confirm current arrangements and costs before sending any stone.

What is the easiest way to sell a diamond I own in Durban?

Start by contacting Prodiam remotely with the certificate details and a description of the stone. The team can give an initial read on trade value and advise on next steps. An in-person Johannesburg appointment may follow for significant-value stones.

Does a national jewellery chain or a national jewellery chain in Durban buy second-hand diamonds?

Chain retailers generally do not purchase diamonds outright from private sellers. Their trade-in programmes, if available, typically apply as credit toward a new purchase rather than a cash offer at trade rate. A specialist dealer like Prodiam gives a cleaner outright cash assessment.
