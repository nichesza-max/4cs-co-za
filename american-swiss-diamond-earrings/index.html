Use this rule

Do not judge one C alone. Read the certificate, inspect the actual stone, then decide whether beauty, budget, or resale confidence matters most.

01

What you are actually buying

Retail chain diamonds are typically purchased in bulk to price points. This means the store can offer accessible prices, but it also means stones are often chosen for cost efficiency rather than optimised cut quality or premium grades. A pair of stud earrings labelled as diamond should carry some documentation of the stones, even if not a full GIA certificate.

02

Comparing chain retail to independent dealers

Chain retailers provide convenience and consistent pricing. Independent dealers and specialist buyers like Prodiam focus on certified natural diamonds and can often offer more transparent grading data and direct sourcing. For earrings with stones above 0.25ct each, a certificate matters for both quality assurance and future resale.

03

What to check when buying diamond earrings

Ask whether the stones are natural or laboratory-grown, what metal the setting is (9ct gold, 18ct gold, or sterling silver), whether there is any certification, and the total carat weight. For studs, also check the butterfly or pushback fitting quality, as loose fittings on diamond studs increase loss risk.

Direct answers

Common questions

Is a national jewellery chain a reputable jeweller in South Africa?

a national jewellery chain is an established retail chain with widespread presence. They operate under consumer protection law including the Consumer Protection Act. Like any retailer, the quality of individual pieces varies.

Do a national jewellery chain diamond earrings have certificates?

Not always. Smaller stones sold at retail price points often do not come with individual grading reports. Ask before buying if certification matters to you.

Are a national jewellery chain diamonds natural or lab-grown?

a national jewellery chain sells both. The type should be disclosed at point of sale. Under the Consumer Protection Act, sellers must not mislead buyers about stone origin.

What is a fair price for diamond stud earrings in South Africa?

Price depends on total carat weight, stone quality, metal type, and whether a certificate is included. Uncertified small-stone studs start low; certified natural diamond studs with 0.50ct total weight and above increase significantly.

Can I sell my a national jewellery chain diamond earrings?

Yes, but resale value depends on the stones and whether they have grading reports. Stones without documentation typically attract lower offers. A specialist buyer like Prodiam can provide an honest valuation.
