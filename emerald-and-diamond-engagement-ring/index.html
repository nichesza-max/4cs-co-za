Short answer

An emerald and diamond ring needs the right stone pairing, not just visual appeal.

Emerald and diamond engagement rings attract buyers who want colour alongside brilliance. The combination requires care: natural emeralds range from Mohs 7.5 to 8.0 and are almost always treated. Knowing what you are buying protects the investment.

Use this rule

Do not judge one C alone. Read the certificate, inspect the actual stone, then decide whether beauty, budget, or resale confidence matters most.

01

Natural emerald characteristics

Natural emeralds are mined primarily in Colombia, Zambia, and Brazil. Zambian emeralds are significant in South African trade. Inclusions in emeralds, called jardin, are expected and do not carry the same stigma as diamond inclusions. Most natural emeralds are fracture-filled with resin or oil to improve clarity.

02

Durability versus a diamond centre

Diamonds sit at Mohs 10 and are the hardest natural material. Emeralds at 7.5 to 8.0 can chip at the edges under impact, particularly if the ring is worn daily and exposed to hard surfaces. A three-claw bezel around the emerald offers better protection than a four-prong open head.

03

Colour and diamond pairing

A vivid green emerald pairs well with near-colourless diamonds (D-H range) in white metal. Pairing with yellow gold can warm the green slightly. Lower-colour diamonds in an emerald ring can create a muddy contrast. The diamonds carry the brilliance; the emerald carries the colour narrative.

04

Certification and treatment disclosure

A reputable seller will disclose treatment type and degree on the gem report. GIA, SSEF, and Gübelin labs issue coloured stone reports for emeralds. Undisclosed fracture-filling is a known trade practice to watch for. Always ask for a lab report on any emerald above half a carat in an engagement ring.

Direct answers

Common questions

Is an emerald and diamond engagement ring durable enough for daily wear?

With a protective setting, yes. A bezel or three-claw setting around the emerald reduces chip risk. The ring should be removed during physical work or sport.

Do natural emeralds always have treatments?

Almost all natural emeralds are treated with oil or resin to reduce visible inclusions. A grading report from a recognised lab will state the treatment type and degree.

What colour diamond pairs best with an emerald?

Near-colourless diamonds in the D-H range provide the cleanest contrast against vivid green. Coloured diamonds are also used intentionally for specific looks.

Can I buy an emerald and diamond engagement ring in Johannesburg?

Yes. Prodiam at Suite F1W6, The Paragon, Bedfordview, handles natural diamonds and can source or advise on coloured stone combinations through its network.

How do I care for an emerald and diamond ring?

Avoid ultrasonic and steam cleaners, which can dissolve or displace fracture fillings. Wipe with a soft damp cloth. Have the ring professionally cleaned and prongs checked annually.

What is the price range for emerald and diamond engagement rings in South Africa?

Entry-level combinations with smaller stones start from a few thousand rand. Vivid, large natural Zambian emeralds paired with high-grade diamonds can run into six figures. Lab-created emeralds cost significantly less.
