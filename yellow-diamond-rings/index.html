Fancy colour diamonds explained

Yellow diamond rings feature stones graded on the fancy colour scale, not the D-Z white diamond scale. The grade and origin determine value.

A yellow diamond ring can contain either a fancy colour natural yellow diamond or a treated or laboratory-grown yellow stone. These are very different products at very different prices. Natural fancy yellow diamonds are graded by intensity, from Fancy Light through to Fancy Vivid. A GIA Fancy Vivid Yellow is among the most valuable yellow stones on the market. A treated or synthetic yellow stone costs a fraction of that.

Use this rule

Do not judge one C alone. Read the certificate, inspect the actual stone, then decide whether beauty, budget, or resale confidence matters most.

01

Natural fancy yellow versus treated yellow

Natural fancy yellow diamonds derive their colour from nitrogen atoms in the crystal structure. The GIA grades their colour intensity: Fancy Light, Fancy, Fancy Intense, Fancy Deep, and Fancy Vivid. Treated yellows are typically near-colourless or light-coloured diamonds that have been irradiated or HPHT-processed to induce colour. A GIA certificate specifies whether the colour is natural or treated. Always ask before buying.

02

Laboratory-grown yellow diamonds

Lab-grown yellow diamonds can be produced in intense yellow colours through HPHT or CVD methods. They are optically identical to natural diamonds and cost significantly less. The question is disclosure. In South Africa, under the Consumer Protection Act, sellers must clearly indicate whether a stone is natural, treated, or laboratory-grown.

03

Setting metal for yellow diamonds

Yellow gold settings complement natural yellow diamonds by blending the stone's colour into the overall warm aesthetic. Rose gold also works. White gold and platinum can provide contrast, making the yellow appear more saturated. Neither choice is wrong, but consider the intended effect.

Direct answers

Common questions

How do I know if a yellow diamond is natural?

A GIA certificate will state whether the colour origin is natural or treated. Treated stones will include a comment noting the colour has been artificially induced. Without a certificate, you cannot determine this reliably by eye.

Are yellow diamonds more expensive than white diamonds?

Natural Fancy Intense and Fancy Vivid yellow diamonds are among the most expensive diamonds per carat globally. However, Fancy Light yellows can be priced comparably to near-colourless whites. Treated and lab-grown yellows are significantly cheaper.

What carat weight is typical for yellow diamond rings in South Africa?

There is no standard. Solitaire rings typically feature centre stones from 0.50ct to 2.00ct and above. Yellow diamonds are often set with white diamond side stones or halos to provide contrast.

Can I sell a yellow diamond ring in South Africa?

Yes. Natural certified fancy yellow diamonds have an active secondary market. A GIA report confirming natural colour origin is essential for achieving a fair price. Prodiam can assess and offer on certified natural yellow stones.

Is yellow gold or white metal better for a yellow diamond?

Neither is objectively better. Yellow gold blends the stone's colour into a warm overall look. White metal makes the yellow colour pop by contrast. Both are common choices depending on personal preference.
