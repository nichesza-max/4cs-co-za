Short answer

Selling a diamond ring in Cape Town: your realistic options.

Cape Town has a number of jewellery retailers, a few independent appraisers, and a limited number of dealers who buy stones from private sellers. Knowing which type of buyer suits your stone keeps you from leaving money behind.

Use this rule

Do not judge one C alone. Read the certificate, inspect the actual stone, then decide whether beauty, budget, or resale confidence matters most.

01

Local options in Cape Town

Cape Town has a retail jewellery district centred around the Waterfront and Long Street, plus a handful of smaller independent jewellers who occasionally buy estate pieces. London Lane Diamonds (LLD) is a Cape Town operator with a buying function. For high-quality certified natural diamonds, the universe of buyers willing to pay at market benchmark is smaller than in Johannesburg, which concentrates the diamond trade.

02

Why JHB specialist dealers are relevant to Cape Town sellers

Johannesburg holds most of South Africa's diamond trading infrastructure. Specialist dealers there have deeper Rapaport access, higher buying volume, and more consistent pricing power than smaller-city operators. For a stone worth R30,000 or more, the difference in offer between a Cape Town generalist and a JHB specialist can easily exceed the cost and inconvenience of a courier transaction.

03

04

What affects the offer regardless of location

The grading certificate, the stone's physical condition, current Rapaport market data, and the cut quality determine the offer. These factors are the same whether you sell in Cape Town or Johannesburg. A GIA-certified natural diamond in good condition with a strong cut grade trades closest to Rapaport benchmark. Uncertified stones and those with lower-demand characteristics trade at a wider discount.

Direct answers

Common questions

Is it safe to courier a diamond ring to a JHB buyer?

Yes, with a SAPS-registered courier, declared value, and insurance cover. Confirm the exact process with the buyer before shipping. Prodiam can guide you through the steps.

Does Prodiam offer a free valuation for Cape Town sellers?

A preliminary assessment based on photographs and certificate details is possible before any stone travels. Contact Prodiam to discuss the stone before committing to shipping.

What insurance do I need when couriering a diamond?

The courier must offer declared-value cover. Read the policy to confirm stones in transit are covered. Some standard courier contracts exclude jewellery without a declared-value rider.

How long does an EFT settlement take from JHB to Cape Town?

Standard EFT between SA banks clears within one business day after the offer is accepted and the agreement is signed.

Should I get a Cape Town appraisal before selling to a JHB buyer?

An independent appraisal gives you a reference point for negotiation. For any stone above R20,000, it is a worthwhile step. Use a GIA-trained gemologist rather than a retail jeweller with a commercial interest.

Can Prodiam buy from sellers outside South Africa?

Prodiam operates in the South African market. Cross-border transactions carry additional regulatory requirements. Contact them directly to discuss your specific situation.
