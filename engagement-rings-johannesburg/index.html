Use this rule

Do not judge one C alone. Read the certificate, inspect the actual stone, then decide whether beauty, budget, or resale confidence matters most.

01

The Sandton retail corridor versus the trade

National mall-based jewellery chains in Sandton, Hyde Park, and Eastgate offer recognisable brand names and polished showrooms. That retail environment comes with layered margin, limited certificate transparency, and settings designed to display well under showroom lighting. The diamond trade operates from commercial workshops in areas like Bedfordview, where manufacturing, certification, and direct sales coexist.

02

What a Johannesburg buyer can access that other cities cannot

Being in Johannesburg means you can visit a manufacturer by appointment, view multiple certified stones side by side, discuss proportion data and cut quality with someone who polishes stones for a living, and negotiate without the overhead of retail branding. This is not a niche option reserved for the trade. Specialist manufacturers see retail buyers by appointment, and the process takes a fraction of the time of mall comparison shopping.

03

How to compare a certified stone properly

Arrive with a GIA grading report number or ask for one before the meeting. Compare table percentage, depth percentage, polish, symmetry, and fluorescence alongside the four Cs. Ask to see the stone in daylight-type lighting, not only in a jeweller's loupe lamp. If the stone cannot be examined on its own before it is set, that is a reason to pause.

Direct answers

Common questions

How far is Bedfordview from Sandton?

Approximately 15 minutes by car outside peak traffic. It sits east of Johannesburg CBD, accessible via the N3 or the M2, making it practical for buyers anywhere in the greater Sandton and Johannesburg north corridor.

What is the price range for a 1ct diamond engagement ring in Johannesburg?

Natural 1ct diamond rings retail across a wide band, roughly R30,000 to R200,000 or more depending on cut, colour, clarity, and where you buy. Manufacturer-direct pricing typically saves 30 to 40 percent versus national mall retail.

Do I need to know what I want before visiting a diamond specialist?

No. A good specialist will start with your priorities, budget, and wearing context, then show you options. Having a rough carat and budget range is enough for a first conversation.

Is GIA the only acceptable diamond certificate in South Africa?

GIA is the most widely recognised benchmark. Other internationally respected labs exist. The key test is whether the certificate is from an independent, internationally audited body rather than an in-house or proprietary grading service.

Can a Johannesburg manufacturer also make a custom setting?

Yes. Manufacturers who work from a workshop can produce custom settings to suit the stone, typically at lower cost than a branded chain because the labour and materials are priced directly.

What is Kimberley Process compliance and why does it matter?

Kimberley Process certification confirms a diamond was not mined to fund conflict. Reputable SA dealers maintain KP compliance as standard. Ask for confirmation if it is not volunteered.
