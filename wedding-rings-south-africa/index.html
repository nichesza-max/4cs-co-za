Short answer

Use this rule

Do not judge one C alone. Read the certificate, inspect the actual stone, then decide whether beauty, budget, or resale confidence matters most.

01

Metal options: gold, white gold and platinum

9-carat gold is 37.5 percent pure gold alloyed for durability. It is the most affordable option and common in everyday-wear bands. 14-carat gold sits at 58.3 percent purity, offering a balance between value and longevity. 18-carat gold is 75 percent pure, has a richer colour, and is the most common premium choice in SA engagement and wedding sets. Platinum is denser, heavier, and more expensive than gold, but is hypoallergenic and does not need replating. White gold, by contrast, requires rhodium replating every two to five years to maintain its colour, because the white finish is a surface treatment, not intrinsic to the metal.

02

Design categories worth knowing

A plain band is the simplest and most durable option. A court or comfort-fit profile has a rounded inner edge that wears more comfortably long-term. Diamond-set bands include channel-set (diamonds recessed in a groove), pavé (stones set close together across the surface), and eternity rings (stones running full circle). An eternity ring cannot be resized without removing stones, which matters if finger size changes. Paired sets, where the engagement ring and wedding band are designed to sit flush together, require buying them from the same jeweller or confirming the profile fits before purchase.

03

Mens and womens wedding rings in SA

Mens wedding rings in SA are typically wider (6 to 8mm) and plainer, though diamond-set mens bands have grown in popularity. Womens rings tend to be narrower (2 to 4mm) and are more commonly set with diamonds. Wedding ring sets, sold as matched pairs, are widely available from both specialist jewellers and chain retailers. The honest comparison: chain retailers offer convenience and familiar brands, but pricing includes franchise overhead and the stone sourcing is less transparent. A specialist jeweller working with a diamond dealer can build a ring to your specification at a comparable or lower price point with full stone documentation.

Direct answers

Common questions

What is the most popular wedding ring metal in South Africa?

18-carat gold is the most common choice for premium rings. 9-carat gold dominates the budget segment because of its durability and lower price point. Platinum has grown in popularity for buyers who want a white metal without the replating maintenance of white gold.

What is the difference between a wedding ring and an engagement ring?

An engagement ring is given at proposal, typically featuring a centre stone. A wedding band is exchanged at the ceremony and is traditionally a plain or lightly set band. In SA, the two are often worn together on the left ring finger, with the wedding band closest to the hand.

Can I resize a diamond eternity ring?

Not easily. An eternity ring with stones running the full circle cannot be resized without removing and repositioning stones, which risks damage and adds cost. Buy to your current finger size and accept that resizing may not be practical.

Is white gold or platinum better for a wedding ring?

Platinum is denser and does not need replating. White gold is less expensive but requires rhodium replating every two to five years to keep its white finish. Over a lifetime of wear, the replating cost narrows the price gap. Both are good choices depending on your budget and maintenance preference.

How much should I spend on a wedding ring in South Africa?

Plain gold bands start from around R3,000 to R8,000 depending on metal and weight. Diamond-set bands in 18-carat gold or platinum can range from R15,000 to R80,000 and above depending on stone quality and total carat weight. The right budget is the one that fits your overall wedding spend without creating post-wedding financial pressure.

Should I buy a wedding ring set or two separate rings?

A matched set guarantees the profiles fit flush. Two separate rings can work if you verify fit before purchase. Buying separately also lets you use different jewellers for the band and the diamond component, which can give you better value on the stone if you source through a specialist dealer.
